# Hemoglobin (Hb)

## Metadata
- Biomarker: hemoglobin
- Category: cbc

## Overview
Hemoglobin is an iron-containing metalloprotein present within human red blood cells. It binds oxygen molecules in the lungs and delivers them to tissue capillaries across all organ systems.

## Clinical Significance
Hemoglobin level is the primary diagnostic indicator for oxygen-carrying capacity and total red blood cell mass. It is essential for evaluating anemia, polycythemia, and systemic oxygenation status.

## Normal Range
- Adult Male: 13.5 - 17.5 g/dL
- Adult Female: 12.0 - 15.5 g/dL

## Low Values (< 12.0 g/dL)
- **Causes**: Iron deficiency, Vitamin B12 or Folate deficiency, active gastrointestinal bleeding, heavy menstruation, chronic kidney disease, or bone marrow disorders.
- **Symptoms**: Chronic fatigue, dyspnea (shortness of breath), pale skin, dizziness, cold peripheral extremities, palpitations.
- **Possible Diseases**: Iron Deficiency Anemia, Megaloblastic Anemia, Anemia of Chronic Disease, Thalassemia.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Consume iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils, beans, dark leafy greens, lean meats) paired with Vitamin C (citrus fruits) to enhance absorption. Avoid tea and coffee during meals.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Seek medical evaluation if hemoglobin falls below 10.0 g/dL or if experiencing severe dizziness and shortness of breath.

## High Values (> 17.5 g/dL)
- **Causes**: Dehydration, chronic hypoxia, smoking, high altitude living, or bone marrow hyperproliferation.
- **Symptoms**: Headaches, dizziness, facial flushing, blurred vision.
- **Possible Diseases**: Polycythemia Vera, Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD), Cyanotic Heart Disease.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Maintain strict fluid hydration (2.5 - 3.5 Liters daily). Quit smoking.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult a hematologist if elevated levels persist after rehydration.
