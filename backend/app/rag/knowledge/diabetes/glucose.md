# Fasting Blood Glucose

## Metadata
- Biomarker: glucose
- Category: diabetes

## Overview
Fasting blood glucose measures the concentration of glucose circulating in blood plasma following an overnight fast of at least 8 hours.

## Clinical Significance
Fasting glucose is the cornerstone diagnostic test for metabolic health, insulin resistance, prediabetes, and Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus.

## Normal Range
- Fasting: 70 - 99 mg/dL

## High Values (>= 100 mg/dL)
- **Prediabetes Range**: 100 - 125 mg/dL
- **Diabetes Mellitus Threshold**: >= 126 mg/dL
- **Causes**: Insulin resistance, pancreatic beta-cell dysfunction, high glycemic diet, physical inactivity, obesity, chronic stress, or corticosteroids.
- **Symptoms**: Polyuria (frequent urination), polydipsia (excessive thirst), polyphagia (excessive hunger), fatigue, blurred vision, slow-healing wounds.
- **Possible Diseases**: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus, Impaired Fasting Glucose, Metabolic Syndrome, Cushing's Syndrome.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Adopt a low-glycemic Mediterranean diet emphasizing non-starchy vegetables, legumes, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit refined sugars and processed carbohydrates. Engage in 150 minutes of weekly moderate aerobic exercise combined with resistance training.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult an endocrinologist if fasting glucose is >= 100 mg/dL on repeated testing or if experiencing diabetic symptoms.

## Low Values (< 70 mg/dL)
- **Causes**: Prolonged fasting, intense un-fueled exercise, insulin secretagogue medication, liver dysfunction, or adrenal insufficiency.
- **Symptoms**: Shakiness, sweating, anxiety, confusion, dizziness, palpitations.
- **Possible Diseases**: Reactive Hypoglycemia, Insulinoma, Adrenal Insufficiency.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Consume 15-20 grams of fast-acting simple carbohydrates (fruit juice, glucose tablets) immediately.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Seek urgent care if blood glucose remains < 55 mg/dL or causes altered consciousness.
