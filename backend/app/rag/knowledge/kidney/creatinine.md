# Serum Creatinine

## Metadata
- Biomarker: creatinine
- Category: kidney

## Overview
Serum creatinine is a metabolic breakdown product of creatine phosphate in muscle tissue. It is filtered by the renal glomeruli and excreted in urine at a constant rate.

## Clinical Significance
Creatinine clearance serves as the primary indicator of glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) and overall kidney excretory function.

## Normal Range
- Adult Male: 0.7 - 1.3 mg/dL
- Adult Female: 0.6 - 1.1 mg/dL

## High Values (> 1.3 mg/dL)
- **Causes**: Acute Kidney Injury (AKI), Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), uncontrolled hypertension, diabetic nephropathy, glomerulonephritis, severe dehydration, or urinary tract obstruction.
- **Symptoms**: Edema (swelling in ankles and feet), puffiness around the eyes, decreased urine volume, nausea, fatigue, metallic taste.
- **Possible Diseases**: Chronic Kidney Disease, Acute Tubular Necrosis, Hypertensive Nephrosclerosis, Diabetic Kidney Disease.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Maintain consistent daily fluid hydration (2.0 - 3.0 Liters of water unless restricted). Avoid nephrotoxic NSAID pain medications (ibuprofen, naproxen). Reduce dietary sodium to < 2,000 mg/day and limit excessive red meat consumption.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult a nephrologist immediately if serum creatinine exceeds 1.5 mg/dL or if experiencing reduced urinary output.

## Low Values (< 0.6 mg/dL)
- **Causes**: Decreased muscle mass, severe malnutrition, advanced age, muscular dystrophy, or pregnancy.
- **Symptoms**: Muscle weakness, weight loss.
- **Possible Diseases**: Sarcopenia, Severe Malnutrition, Muscular Atrophy.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Ensure adequate dietary protein intake and participate in regular progressive resistance exercise.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult a doctor if low creatinine is accompanied by rapid unexplained weight loss.
