# Total Cholesterol

## Metadata
- Biomarker: cholesterol
- Category: lipid

## Overview
Total cholesterol measures the sum of circulating lipoprotein particles including low-density lipoproteins (LDL), high-density lipoproteins (HDL), and very-low-density lipoproteins (VLDL).

## Clinical Significance
Elevated total cholesterol is a major modifiable risk factor for atherosclerosis, coronary artery disease, and ischemic stroke.

## Normal Range
- Desirable: < 200 mg/dL

## High Values (>= 200 mg/dL)
- **Borderline High**: 200 - 239 mg/dL
- **High Risk**: >= 240 mg/dL
- **Causes**: High saturated and trans fat intake, physical inactivity, obesity, familial hypercholesterolemia, hypothyroidism, or nephrotic syndrome.
- **Symptoms**: Asymptomatic in early stages; long-term vascular accumulation causes chest pain (angina), claudication, or cardiovascular events.
- **Possible Diseases**: Hypercholesterolemia, Coronary Artery Disease, Atherosclerosis.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Limit saturated fat intake to < 7% of total daily calories. Eliminate artificial trans fats. Increase intake of soluble viscous fiber (oats, barley, psyllium, beans) and plant sterols. Perform 150 minutes per week of aerobic exercise.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult a cardiologist or physician for comprehensive lipid panel (LDL, HDL, Triglycerides, ApoB) evaluation.

## Low Values (< 120 mg/dL)
- **Causes**: Hyperthyroidism, malabsorption syndromes, severe liver failure, or chronic malnutrition.
- **Symptoms**: Fatigue, weight loss.
- **Possible Diseases**: Malabsorption, Severe Hepatic Insufficiency.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Discuss low cholesterol levels with a doctor if accompanied by chronic gastrointestinal issues or weight loss.
