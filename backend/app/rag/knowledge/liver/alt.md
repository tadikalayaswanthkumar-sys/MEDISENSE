# Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT)

## Metadata
- Biomarker: alt
- Category: liver

## Overview
ALT (also known as SGPT) is an enzyme concentrated inside liver cells (hepatocytes). It catalyzes the transfer of amino groups in protein metabolism.

## Clinical Significance
Serum ALT level is the most specific laboratory marker for acute hepatocellular injury and active liver inflammation.

## Normal Range
- Adult: 7 - 56 U/L

## High Values (> 56 U/L)
- **Causes**: Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/MASLD), alcoholic hepatitis, viral hepatitis (Hepatitis B/C), medication hepatotoxicity (acetaminophen overconsumption, statins), or biliary tract obstruction.
- **Symptoms**: Fatigue, right upper quadrant abdominal aching, jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes), dark urine, abdominal distension.
- **Possible Diseases**: Fatty Liver Disease, Viral Hepatitis, Toxic Hepatitis, Cirrhosis, Cholecystitis.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Completely avoid alcohol consumption. Stop taking unverified herbal supplements. Adopt an antioxidant-dense Mediterranean diet low in saturated fats and refined sugars. Engage in regular physical activity to reduce liver fat accumulation.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult a gastroenterologist/hepatologist if ALT is > 100 U/L or accompanied by jaundice.

## Low Values (< 7 U/L)
- **Causes**: Pyridoxal phosphate (Vitamin B6) deficiency, advanced chronic kidney disease, or urinary tract infection.
- **Symptoms**: Asymptomatic in most cases.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Ensure adequate dietary Vitamin B6 intake (poultry, fish, potatoes, non-citrus fruits).
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Discuss with a doctor if accompanied by signs of nutritional deficiency.
