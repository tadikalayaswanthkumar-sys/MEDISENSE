# Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH)

## Metadata
- Biomarker: tsh
- Category: thyroid

## Overview
TSH is a glycoprotein hormone synthesized by the anterior pituitary gland. It stimulates the thyroid gland to produce thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3).

## Clinical Significance
TSH is the primary screening parameter for thyroid gland dysregulation (hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism).

## Normal Range
- Adult: 0.45 - 4.5 uIU/mL

## High Values (> 4.5 uIU/mL)
- **Causes**: Primary hypothyroidism, Hashimoto's thyroiditis, iodine deficiency, surgical thyroid removal, or pituitary TSH hypersecretion.
- **Symptoms**: Unexplained weight gain, chronic fatigue, cold intolerance, dry skin, constipation, depression, memory sluggishness.
- **Possible Diseases**: Primary Hypothyroidism, Hashimoto's Autoimmune Thyroiditis.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Ensure adequate dietary iodine and selenium intake. Manage chronic stress and maintain adequate sleep hygiene.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult an endocrinologist for Free T4 and thyroid peroxidase (TPO) antibody testing.

## Low Values (< 0.45 uIU/mL)
- **Causes**: Graves' disease, toxic multinodular goiter, thyroiditis, or excessive thyroid hormone medication dosage.
- **Symptoms**: Tachycardia (rapid heartbeat), unexplained weight loss, heat intolerance, anxiety, tremors, frequent bowel movements.
- **Possible Diseases**: Hyperthyroidism, Graves' Disease, Toxic Nodular Goiter.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Avoid caffeine and stimulants. Ensure adequate hydration.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult an endocrinologist promptly if experiencing palpitations or unexplained rapid weight loss.
