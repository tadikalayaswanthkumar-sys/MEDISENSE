# Vitamin D (25-Hydroxyvitamin D)

## Metadata
- Biomarker: vitamin_d
- Category: vitamins

## Overview
Vitamin D (25-OH) is a fat-soluble prohormone essential for intestinal calcium absorption, skeletal bone mineralization, and immune cell regulation.

## Clinical Significance
Vitamin D status is crucial for preventing rickets in children, osteomalacia/osteoporosis in adults, and maintaining immunocompetence.

## Normal Range
- Optimal: 30 - 100 ng/mL

## Low Values (< 30 ng/mL)
- **Insufficiency**: 20 - 29 ng/mL
- **Deficiency**: < 20 ng/mL
- **Causes**: Inadequate sunlight exposure, dark skin pigmentation, malabsorption disorders (Celiac disease, Crohn's disease), obesity, or renal failure.
- **Symptoms**: Bone pain, muscle weakness, frequent infections, fatigue, mood changes (depression).
- **Possible Diseases**: Vitamin D Deficiency, Osteomalacia, Osteoporosis, Rickets.
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Obtain 15-20 minutes of daily midday solar exposure on arms and legs. Eat fatty fish (salmon, tuna, sardines), egg yolks, fortified dairy products, and mushrooms. Perform weight-bearing exercise to preserve bone density.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Consult a doctor to assess bone mineral density (DEXA scan) and determine proper supplementation dosage.

## High Values (> 100 ng/mL)
- **Causes**: Excessive exogenous Vitamin D supplementation.
- **Symptoms**: Hypercalcemia, nausea, confusion, kidney stones, excessive thirst.
- **Possible Diseases**: Vitamin D Toxicity, Hypercalcemia.
- **When to Consult a Doctor**: Stop high-dose supplements immediately and consult a physician if Vitamin D exceeds 100 ng/mL.
