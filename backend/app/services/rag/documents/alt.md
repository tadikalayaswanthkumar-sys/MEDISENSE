# Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT) Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: alt
- Category: Liver Function
- Unit: U/L
- Normal Range: 7 - 56 U/L

## Clinical Interpretation
ALT is an enzyme predominantly found inside liver cells (hepatocytes). Elevated serum ALT signals liver cell inflammation or damage.

## Elevated Serum ALT (Hepatocellular Injury Risk)
- **Moderate Elevation**: 57 - 150 U/L
- **High Elevation**: > 150 U/L
- **Possible Causes**: Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/MASLD), alcoholic liver disease, viral hepatitis (Hepatitis B/C), drug-induced liver injury (acetaminophen overconsumption, statins), or gallbladder disease.
- **Symptoms**: Right upper quadrant abdominal discomfort, fatigue, jaundice (yellowing of eyes/skin), dark urine, abdominal bloating.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Strictly refrain from alcohol consumption. Discontinue unverified dietary herbal supplements that cause hepatotoxicity.
- **Diet**: Adopt a plant-forward Mediterranean diet rich in antioxidants, coffee (associated with hepatoprotection), green vegetables, and low in saturated fats and refined fructose.
- **Exercise**: Engage in regular physical activity (cardio + weight training) to reduce intrahepatic fat accumulation.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult a gastroenterologist/hepatologist for hepatic ultrasound, viral hepatitis panel, and liver stiffness assessment.
