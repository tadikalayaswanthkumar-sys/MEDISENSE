# Total Cholesterol Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: cholesterol
- Category: Lipid Profile
- Unit: mg/dL
- Normal Range: 125 - 199 mg/dL

## Clinical Interpretation
Total cholesterol measures the combination of LDL, HDL, and VLDL cholesterol circulating in the blood stream.

## Elevated Total Cholesterol (Hypercholesterolemia Risk)
- **Borderline High**: 200 - 239 mg/dL
- **High Risk**: >= 240 mg/dL
- **Possible Causes**: Familial hypercholesterolemia, diet rich in saturated and trans fats, physical inactivity, obesity, hypothyroidism, or nephrotic syndrome.
- **Symptoms**: Asymptomatic in early stages; long-term elevation leads to arterial plaque buildup (atherosclerosis), increasing angina, heart attack, and stroke risk.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Quit smoking. Maintain healthy body mass index (BMI).
- **Diet**: Consume soluble viscous fiber (oats, barley, psyllium, beans), plant sterols, nuts, and omega-3 rich fatty fish (salmon, mackerel). Limit saturated fats to < 7% of total daily calorie intake.
- **Exercise**: Perform at least 30 minutes of aerobic exercise (walking, swimming, running) 5 days per week to raise protective HDL and lower atherogenic lipids.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult a cardiologist or general physician for advanced lipid testing (ApoB, Lp(a)) and cardiovascular risk stratification.
