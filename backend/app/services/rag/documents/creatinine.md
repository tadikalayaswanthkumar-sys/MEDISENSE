# Serum Creatinine Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: creatinine
- Category: Kidney Function
- Unit: mg/dL
- Normal Range: 0.6 - 1.2 mg/dL

## Clinical Interpretation
Creatinine is a chemical waste product derived from muscle breakdown. Healthy kidneys filter creatinine from the blood and excrete it in urine.

## Elevated Serum Creatinine (Renal Impairment Risk)
- **Borderline Range**: 1.3 - 2.0 mg/dL
- **Severe Renal Impairment Threshold**: > 2.0 mg/dL
- **Possible Causes**: Impaired glomerular filtration, Acute Kidney Injury (AKI), Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), uncontrolled hypertension, diabetic nephropathy, dehydration, or urinary tract obstruction.
- **Symptoms**: Fluid retention (edema in ankles/legs), puffiness around eyes, decreased urinary output, fatigue, nausea, metal taste in mouth.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Maintain strict daily fluid hydration (2 - 3 Liters of water unless restricted by physician). Avoid over-the-counter nephrotoxic medications like non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs - ibuprofen, naproxen).
- **Diet**: Limit high sodium, high potassium, and excessive animal protein intake. Reduce processed meats and sodium to < 2,000 mg/day.
- **Exercise**: Engage in low-impact walking; avoid extreme unaccustomed heavy weightlifting that causes acute rhabdomyolysis.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult a nephrologist for eGFR calculation, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (uACR), and renal ultrasound.
