# Fasting Blood Glucose Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: glucose
- Category: Metabolic Panel
- Unit: mg/dL
- Normal Range: 70 - 99 mg/dL

## Clinical Interpretation
Fasting blood glucose measures the concentration of glucose in the bloodstream after an overnight fast of at least 8 hours.

## High Fasting Glucose (Hyperglycemia / Diabetes Risk)
- **Prediabetes Range**: 100 - 125 mg/dL
- **Diabetes Mellitus Threshold**: >= 126 mg/dL
- **Possible Causes**: Insulin resistance, pancreatic beta-cell dysfunction, metabolic syndrome, sedentary lifestyle, high glycemic diet, stress, or corticosteroids.
- **Symptoms**: Increased thirst (polydipsia), frequent urination (polyuria), unexplained weight loss, blurred vision, fatigue.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Limit refined carbohydrates, sugary beverages, and processed foods. Prioritize fiber-rich whole foods.
- **Diet**: Adopt a low-glycemic index Mediterranean diet emphasizing non-starchy vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and healthy fats.
- **Exercise**: Perform 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise per week (e.g. brisk walking, cycling) combined with twice-weekly resistance training to improve insulin sensitivity.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult an endocrinologist or primary care physician for HbA1c testing and comprehensive metabolic evaluation.

## Low Fasting Glucose (Hypoglycemia)
- **Hypoglycemia Threshold**: < 70 mg/dL
- **Possible Causes**: Prolonged fasting, intense un-fueled exercise, liver dysfunction, or endocrine insufficiency.
- **Symptoms**: Shakiness, sweating, confusion, irritability, dizziness, sweating.
- **Dietary Intervention**: Consume 15-20 grams of fast-acting simple carbohydrates (e.g., fruit juice) immediately and retest blood glucose after 15 minutes.
