# Hemoglobin (Hb) Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: hemoglobin
- Category: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
- Unit: g/dL
- Normal Range: 13.5 - 17.5 g/dL (Male), 12.0 - 15.5 g/dL (Female)

## Clinical Interpretation
Hemoglobin is the iron-rich protein in red blood cells that transports oxygen from the lungs to tissues throughout the body.

## Low Hemoglobin (Anemia)
- **Possible Causes**: Iron deficiency, Vitamin B12 or Folate deficiency, chronic blood loss, gastrointestinal bleeding, chronic kidney disease, or bone marrow disorders.
- **Symptoms**: Fatigue, shortness of breath, pale skin, dizziness, cold hands and feet, rapid heartbeat.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Avoid caffeine during meals as it inhibits iron absorption.
- **Diet**: Consume iron-rich foods including spinach, lentils, beans, dark leafy greens, fortified cereals, lean red meat, and pair with Vitamin C (citrus fruits) to enhance non-heme iron uptake.
- **Exercise**: Engage in light to moderate aerobic exercise as tolerated; avoid strenuous overexertion until levels normalize.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult a primary physician or hematologist for complete iron study (ferritin, TIBC) and peripheral blood smear.

## High Hemoglobin (Polycythemia)
- **Possible Causes**: Chronic hypoxia, heavy smoking, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), high altitude living, or dehydration.
- **Symptoms**: Headaches, dizziness, blurred vision, flushed skin.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Maintain strict daily fluid hydration (2.5 - 3.5 Liters of water). Stop smoking.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult a physician for arterial oxygen saturation and erythropoietin testing.
