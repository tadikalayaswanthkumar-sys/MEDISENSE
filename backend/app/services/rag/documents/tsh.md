# Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: tsh
- Category: Thyroid Function
- Unit: uIU/mL
- Normal Range: 0.45 - 4.5 uIU/mL

## Clinical Interpretation
TSH is produced by the pituitary gland to regulate the synthesis of thyroid hormones (T3 and T4) by the thyroid gland.

## Elevated TSH (Hypothyroidism Risk)
- **Threshold**: > 4.5 uIU/mL
- **Possible Causes**: Primary hypothyroidism, Hashimoto's thyroiditis, iodine deficiency, or pituitary hypersecretion.
- **Symptoms**: Weight gain, chronic fatigue, cold intolerance, dry skin, hair thinning, constipation, memory sluggishness.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Manage chronic stress, ensure adequate sleep, and ensure adequate dietary selenium and zinc intake.
- **Diet**: Consume iodine-sufficient balanced diet (seafood, dairy, iodized salt). Limit raw goitrogenic vegetables (cabbage, kale) in large quantities if iodine deficient.
- **Exercise**: Perform regular moderate physical activity to stimulate basal metabolic rate.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult an endocrinologist for Free T4, Free T3, and anti-TPO thyroid antibody laboratory testing.

## Suppressed TSH (Hyperthyroidism Risk)
- **Threshold**: < 0.45 uIU/mL
- **Possible Causes**: Graves' disease, toxic nodular goiter, thyroiditis, or excessive exogenous thyroid hormone therapy.
- **Symptoms**: Rapid heartbeat (tachycardia), unexplained weight loss, heat intolerance, nervousness, hand tremors.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Seek medical evaluation for thyroid receptor antibodies and thyroid uptake scan.
