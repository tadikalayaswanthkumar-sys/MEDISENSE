# Vitamin D (25-Hydroxyvitamin D) Clinical Reference Document

## Metadata
- Biomarker: vitamin_d
- Category: Vitamins & Minerals
- Unit: ng/mL
- Normal Range: 30 - 100 ng/mL

## Clinical Interpretation
Vitamin D (25-OH) is essential for intestinal calcium absorption, bone mineralization, immune system function, and neuromuscular health.

## Low Vitamin D (Deficiency Risk)
- **Insufficiency**: 20 - 29 ng/mL
- **Deficiency**: < 20 ng/mL
- **Possible Causes**: Limited sunlight exposure, dark skin pigmentation, malabsorption syndromes (Celiac, Crohn's), renal or hepatic impairment, or inadequate dietary intake.
- **Symptoms**: Bone pain, muscle weakness, fatigue, frequent infections, mood changes.
- **Lifestyle Guidance**: Get 15-20 minutes of midday sunlight exposure on arms and legs 3 times weekly.
- **Diet**: Eat fatty fish (salmon, tuna), egg yolks, fortified milk, yogurt, and mushrooms.
- **Exercise**: Weight-bearing exercises (walking, jogging, strength training) to preserve bone mineral density.
- **Doctor Consultation Advice**: Consult a doctor to evaluate bone density (DEXA scan) and determine optimal supplementation dosage.
